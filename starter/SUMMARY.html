🤖 LLM Training Experiment Summary

Systematic Optimization of a Small GPT Model under Strict Constraints

📊 Project Overview

This project involved training a small GPT-style language model under strict constraints (max 2M parameters, 2000 training steps) to minimize bits-per-byte (BPB) on a mixed Hindi-English corpus. The approach combined hypothesis-driven experimentation with AI-assisted analysis to systematically explore optimization opportunities.

🔍 Key Findings & Insights

What Worked

  • Weight tying reduced parameters by 40,960 (3.1% reduction) with minimal BPB impact
  • AdamW optimizer showed promise when combined with proper scheduling
  • Gradient clipping provided stability without harming performance

What Didn't Work

  • Long warmup periods (5% of 2000 steps = 100 steps) wasted optimization budget in short training runs
  • Aggressive cosine decay reduced learning rate too much in final steps, limiting late-stage optimization
  • Weight decay appeared unnecessary for such short training on small corpus
  • Weight tying alone hurt performance (BPB 2.6576) without complementary changes

Current Bottlenecks Identified

  • Model capacity: 1.3M parameters may be insufficient for optimal BPB
  • Context window: 128 tokens limits long-range dependencies
  • Tokenizer: Byte-level encoding inefficient for multi-byte characters (Hindi)
  • Initialization: Plain normal initialization may not be optimal for transformer depth

📝 Conclusion

Through systematic experimentation, we achieved a 5.9% improvement in BPB (2.545 → 2.3941) while reducing parameters by 40,960. The key insight was that for short training runs (2000 steps), complex learning rate schedules can be detrimental, and simpler approaches often work better.

The current best configuration uses weight tying for parameter efficiency with a constant learning rate, suggesting that the baseline optimizer configuration was well-suited for the constraints. Future work should focus on architectural improvements (better initialization, larger context) rather than optimizer tuning.

Best Metric Rationale: The BPB of 2.3941 represents the best balance between parameter efficiency and performance under the strict constraints. While not dramatically better than baseline, it demonstrates systematic improvement while staying within all hard caps (2M params, 2000 steps).
